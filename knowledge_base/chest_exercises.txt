Chest exercises: key movements and coaching cues

Push-ups
- Primary muscles: pectoralis major (chest), triceps, anterior deltoids.
- Secondary/stabilizers: serratus anterior, core (abdominals/obliques), glutes.
- Form cues: hands slightly wider than shoulders, elbows ~30–45° from torso, body in a straight line, chest touches near hands.
- Common mistakes: hips sagging, flared elbows, incomplete range of motion.
- Progressions: incline push-ups (easier) → standard → decline/weighted (harder).

Bench press (barbell)
- Primary muscles: chest, triceps, anterior deltoids.
- Form cues: shoulder blades retracted/down, feet planted, bar touches mid-lower chest, keep wrists stacked.
- Safety: use a spotter when heavy; avoid bouncing the bar.

Incline dumbbell press
- Emphasis: upper chest (clavicular head) plus shoulders/triceps.
- Form cues: set bench to a moderate incline (~20–35°), control the descent, press up and slightly in.

Chest fly (dumbbell/cable/machine)
- Emphasis: chest isolation.
- Form cues: slight elbow bend, move through a comfortable range, focus on a controlled stretch.
