Yoga poses and their benefits

Mountain Pose (Tadasana)
- Alignment: stand tall with feet hip-width apart, weight evenly distributed, thighs engaged, shoulders relaxed, chin neutral.
- Benefits: improves posture and balance, builds awareness of alignment, gentle activation for legs and core.
- When to use: as a starting pose, reset between standing flows, or to practice mindful breathing.

Downward-Facing Dog (Adho Mukha Svanasana)
- Alignment: hands shoulder-width apart, fingers spread, press through palms; hips lift up and back to form an inverted “V”; knees can be slightly bent.
- Benefits: lengthens hamstrings and calves, opens shoulders, strengthens arms and core, encourages blood flow toward the upper body.
- When to use: warm-up for full-body sessions, active recovery between strength sets, or part of a mobility flow.

Warrior II (Virabhadrasana II)
- Alignment: front knee bent over ankle, back leg straight, hips open to the side, arms extended at shoulder height, gaze over front hand.
- Benefits: strengthens legs and glutes, opens hips, improves stability and endurance, trains focus and breath under tension.
- When to use: lower-body or yoga-inspired conditioning days, as a hold for strength/endurance or in flowing transitions.

Tree Pose (Vrikshasana)
- Alignment: stand on one leg, place the other foot on calf or inner thigh (avoid the knee), hands at heart or overhead, keep hips squared forward.
- Benefits: improves single-leg balance, ankle and foot stability, and concentration; helpful for joint control and proprioception.
- When to use: balance training, joint-prep before running or sports, or as a calming focus drill at the end of a workout.

Child’s Pose (Balasana)
- Alignment: kneel, sit back toward heels, fold torso over thighs, arms forward or by sides, forehead relaxed toward the mat.
- Benefits: gently stretches lower back, hips, and ankles; encourages relaxation of the neck and shoulders; calms breathing.
- When to use: between challenging sets or poses, active rest during yoga flows, or as a cool-down / recovery posture.

Cat–Cow (Marjaryasana–Bitilasana)
- Alignment: on hands and knees, alternate between arching (cow) and rounding (cat) the spine with breath.
- Benefits: mobilizes the entire spine, warms up back and core muscles, reduces stiffness from sitting.
- When to use: pre-workout warm-up, especially for back and core sessions, or as a gentle morning mobility routine.

Bridge Pose (Setu Bandhasana)
- Alignment: lying on back, knees bent, feet hip-width, press into feet to lift hips; avoid overarching neck, keep knees tracking over feet.
- Benefits: strengthens glutes and hamstrings, engages posterior chain, gently opens hip flexors and chest.
- When to use: glute activation, low-impact posterior-chain work, or as a bridge (pun intended) between stretching and strength work.
