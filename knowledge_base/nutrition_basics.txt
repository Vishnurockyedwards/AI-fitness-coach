Nutrition basics for training and recovery

Calories
- To lose weight: maintain a small calorie deficit over time.
- To gain muscle: aim for a modest surplus with sufficient protein and progressive training.

Protein
- Supports muscle repair and growth.
- Practical guideline: include protein at each meal (e.g., eggs, yogurt, chicken, tofu, lentils).

Carbohydrates
- Primary fuel for hard training.
- Helpful around workouts for energy and performance (e.g., rice, oats, potatoes, fruit).

Fats
- Important for hormones and overall health.
- Include quality sources (e.g., olive oil, nuts, seeds, fatty fish, avocado).

Hydration
- Dehydration reduces performance.
- Drink regularly; increase fluids with heat/sweat and higher training volume.

Simple pre/post workout ideas
- Pre: banana + yogurt; oats; toast + peanut butter.
- Post: protein + carbs (e.g., chicken + rice; tofu + noodles; whey + fruit).
